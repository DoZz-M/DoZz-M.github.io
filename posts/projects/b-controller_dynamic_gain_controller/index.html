    B-Control: parameter line based dynamic gain controller

    Motivation#

    A controller, essentially a mapping from current known information to next time output, utilizes system state be it to observer or measured and user input to make decisions. However, relying solely on a constant gain for this mapping seems to be not optimal. For example, a big control gain means small steady state error and fast responses to control system, but has bigger overshoot and make actuator easier to saturation, while small gain implies big steady state error, slow response, even though it fix problem of saturation and overshoot. In such cases, a variable gain approach that adapts to different conditions might be more effective to achieve optimal results.

    Bezier curves, celebrated for their elegance and versatility in the world of computer graphics, provide a compelling framework for modeling gain variation across system states. These curves are defined by a set of control points that collectively sculpt the curve’s shape, elegantly dictating the gain characteristics. However, identifying the optimal arrangement of these control points poses a challenge that calls for sophisticated optimization techniques.

    To do that, LQR like object with multi-objective optimization emerges as a harmonious approach to discovering the optimal arrangement of control points, a symphony of objectives blending stability, performance, and energy-efficiency. The degree evaluation property of Bezier curves empowers us to adaptively refine the curve during optimization. This remarkable property allows us to strategically introduce additional control points when Pareto front stuck, effectively guiding the search toward more desirable solutions. It also provides a natural convergence criterion: when adding extra points no longer yields performance improvements, we have strong evidence that we’ve reached an optimal configuration.

    Future work may focus on:

    2. Control efficiency analysis.
    3. Combing with sliding surface design.
